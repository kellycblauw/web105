The Lord of the Rings

The Lord of the Rings Trilogy was filmed entirely in New Zealand.

Fans of The Lord of the Rings can visit several locations in Wellington such as, the Shire, Gardens of Isengard, Rivendell, Osgiliath Wood, and the Paths of the Dead.

Fans can visit the real Village of Hobbiton in the Shire, as it remains as a permanent attraction. Other locations can also be found throughout New Zealand, but not all are as accessible as Hobbiton.

Māori Culture

Tourists can see Māori culture close up and even participate in special ceremonies to experience their traditions for themselves.

You can also visit the marae, the Māori neeting grounds. According to 100% Pure New Zealand, "A visitor who has never set foot on a marae is known as waewae tapu or sacred feet. They must partake in a formal welcoming ceremony, called a pōwhiri, to remove the tapu (sacredness) and become one people with those of the marae." Even if you choose not to participate in these traditions, you will likely come across a marae as you make your way through New Zealand.

Volcanic & Geothermal Nature

In the Rotorua areas, tourists can watch boiling mud pools and geysers with heat beneath their feet from the ground filled with steam. According to 100% Pure New Zealand, "Minutes from the city centre, geysers of boiling water roar from the ground and pools of bubbling mud gurgle and belch." There are plenty of walkways that offer easy acces and views of this interesting bit of nature.

Whale Watching

Tourists can visit Auckland and watch whales in their natural environment in the Hauraki Gulf Marine Park. Whales aren't the only wildlife visitors can expect to see here as "over 25 of the 37 southern hemisphere marine mammals have been identified in the area, which makes up almost a third of the world’s population of these species", according to 100% Pure New Zealand.

Kaikoura is one of the one places in the world where you can see sperm whales. According to 100% Pure New Zealand, "Whales thrive close to Kaikoura because of its unusual submarine landscape. The continental shelf drops quickly into a number of extremely deep underwater canyons. In addition, a warm current from the north meets a colder one from the south. This causes nutrients from deep within the ocean to be carried upward, a phenomenon that helps to support all types of marine life from plankton and krill to dolphins and whales."
