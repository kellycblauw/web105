What D&D Means to Me


Allowing myself to be fully immersed in the endless worlds of Dungeons and Dragons has impacted my creativity and confidence in a really amazing way. It has also made the bonds of friendship within our group stronger, and more interesting.


There are rules, but...


One of my favorite parts of D&D is having the ability to change the rules and make it your own. Standard rules exist, but it really comes down to whatever your dungeon master decides. This makes the content of the game everchanging and exciting. If your character begins to change over time, or if you simply want to take your story in a different direction, you can tweak or take up special skills to better suit your needs.

D&D in Pop Culture


Mike, Dustin and Lucas can be seen playing Dungeons and Dragons in the tv series, Stranger Things. It's really cool to see D&D being normalized through pop culture, because it's been criticized in the past.


In addition to movies and tv, popular video games have incorporated D&D into their storylines. Borderlands 2 features DLC where Tiny Tina has the player go on an adventure based around her own tabletop game.
