Titanic Review

Summary

The Titanic directed and written by James Cameron is about a a woman, Rose, retelling the story of her time aboard the ship, Titanic. She tells a story of great adventure, love and death that spanned her 3 days spent on the great ship which abruptly ends before their arrival to America. Rose luckily made it to tell her story, but many lives were lost when the ship sank after colliding with a glacier.

Rose is beckoned by a search team that is looking for a lost piece of jewelry after she insists the special necklace once belonged to her. Once Rose meets with the team, she begins recalling those precious and saddening moments.

Love and Loss

The Titanic is about more than just a voyage interrupted by chaos, but it is a modern day Rome and Juliet love story. Rose comes from a wealthy family, and is set to marry a man within her social class. This is not at all what Rose actually wants for herself, but she is pressured by her mother to further secure their wealth. Rose can't stand the pressure or lack of choices she is left with and intends to take her own life until Jack stumbles upon the scene.

Jack comes to the rescue and talks her off the ledge in a smooth and calculated way. This begins the start of their love story. Their connection is apparent through their dialogue and in the way the film is shot. It does a wonderful job of showing how their relationship develops, even with the obstacles thrown in their way. Jack is from the lower class, and only ended up aboard the Titanic by chance. Their differences matter not to Rose, and she pursues him regardless. Throughout the movie, Jack takes Rose on a wild adventure and she gets to experience a different life. Rose and Jack only knew eachother for a few days, but he would end giving his life for hers when the Titanic inevitably sinks.

These experiences changed Rose. Jack showed her she could be anything she wanted, and she ultimately chooses a different life. She vividly expresses her gratitude for this in this quote from the Titanic, "A woman's heart is a deep ocean of secrets. But now you know there was a man named Jack Dawson and that he saved me... in every way that a person can be saved." It may seem like she is speaking literally, but it is clear that she is also addressing her way of life. Rose is shown safely in America after being rescued, and she intentionally avoids her mother - clearly indicating she will not be returning to that life. At the end of the movie, the famous necklace, Heart of the Ocean, is returned to Rose and she tosses it right back into the ocean where it belonged. The necklace represented that old life she wittingly left behind and it would have to stay there.
