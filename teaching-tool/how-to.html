How to:

LIGHT

Most succulents require at least half a day of sunlight. In very hot areas, some shade may be recommended. If your succulents aren't getting enough sun, they will actually begin to grow and stretch in order to find more light. It is also useful to rotate your succulents frequently to ensure the entire plant is getting enough sun and it will help it to stand straight.

WATER

When watering your succulents, pour water directly into the soil until it runs through the drainage holes. Do NOT use a spray bottle for watering succulents, as it can lead to moldy leaves and frail roots. Allow the soil to dry slightly between waterings.

SOIL

Succulents require good draining soil. If you're planting is pot or container, you will want to make sure your planter has draining holes. You can also incorporate sand with your soil, or place crushed rocks at the bottom of the container to promote better drainage.

PLACEMENT

Succulents are often crammed together in little planters or containers, but this creates an overcrowded environment for the little plants and will likely lead to mold or insect infestations. Additionally, it will make it harder for each of the plants to survive on limited food and water they'll be sharing in the little space. You can easily remove and replant your succulent in a spacious environment.

As far as plants go, succulents are among the easier to care for. There are plenty of different types with a broad range of shape and color, so you will definitely be able to find one that appeals to you.
