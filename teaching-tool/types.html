Types

There are hundreds of different types of succulents and cacti, but these are some of my favorites.


Acanthocereus tetragonus, Fairy Castle Cactus



According to, Succulents and Sunshine, the Fairy Castle Cactus grows branches that clump together vertically, forming the turrets of a “fairy castle.” It grows well indoors, and is perfect for beginners.

Adromischus maculatus, ‘Calico Hearts’








The Calico Hearts succulent is perfect for beginners, requiring minimal care, it will do fine indoors in bright light.

Aeonium arboreum, 'Zwartkop'






According to, Succulents and Sunshine, the Zwartkop branches off in clumps, becoming bushy when allowed to grow and spread outside. It provides excellent contrast to lighter colored succulents in arrangements.

Agave parryi truncata, Artichoke Agave








According to, Succulents and Sunshine, this artichoke-shaped agave has beautiful blue-gray leaves with sharp spines running along the ridges. Each leaf is tipped with a long dark spike. Although it is a slow-grower, it can grow up to four feet wide. Watch for yellow flowers in mature specimens in the summer. It is monocarpic, but will produce offsets that continue growing after the mother plant has died.
